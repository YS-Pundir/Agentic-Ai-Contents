# Pre-Read: The EDA Checklist

## What You’ll Learn
In this pre-read, you’ll discover:
- What **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** is and why it comes before modeling  
- The **standard steps** analysts follow during EDA  
- How to identify **outliers** and unusual data points  
- How **correlation heatmaps** reveal relationships between variables  
- How to turn analysis into a **clear data story**

---

## Introduction: Before Modeling, You Must Explore
Imagine receiving a new dataset with thousands of rows.

Jumping directly into building a model would be risky because you might not know:
- If values are missing  
- If numbers look unrealistic  
- If variables are related  

That’s why analysts begin with **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**.

EDA is the process of **investigating and understanding data before making conclusions or building models**.

---

## Why EDA Matters
EDA helps you:

1. **Understand the structure of the dataset**  
2. **Detect errors and unusual patterns**  
3. **Discover relationships between variables**

Skipping EDA often leads to incorrect conclusions or poor models.

---

## From Raw Data to Insight
### What You Already Know
You can:
- Load datasets with Pandas  
- Inspect rows and columns  
- Create visualizations  

But raw data still hides patterns.

### The EDA Approach
EDA adds a structured process:
- Inspect → Clean → Explore → Summarize

This turns a dataset into meaningful insight.

---

## Core Steps in an EDA Workflow
### 1. Standard EDA Steps
A typical EDA checklist includes:

- Inspect the dataset structure  
- Check for missing values  
- Review distributions of variables  
- Visualize key relationships  

These steps build a strong understanding before analysis.

---

### 2. Identifying Outliers
**Outliers** are data points that differ significantly from others.

Examples include:
- Extremely high prices  
- Impossible ages  
- Unusual measurements  

Outliers may indicate:
- Errors in data collection  
- Rare but important cases  

Detecting them early is essential.

---

### 3. Correlation Heatmaps
A **correlation heatmap** visualizes relationships between variables.

It helps answer questions like:
- Do two variables increase together?  
- Does one variable decrease as another increases?  

Heatmaps make relationships visible at a glance.

---

### 4. Building a Data Story
EDA isn’t just exploration—it’s about forming a **narrative**.

A good data story explains:
- What patterns were found  
- What anomalies appeared  
- What relationships matter most  

This story guides future analysis and modeling.

---

## Putting It All Together
A typical EDA workflow:
1. Inspect dataset structure  
2. Identify missing values and anomalies  
3. Explore distributions and correlations  
4. Visualize key relationships  
5. Summarize insights into a clear narrative  

This prepares data for deeper analysis or modeling.
