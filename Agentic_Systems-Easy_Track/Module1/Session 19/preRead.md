## Introduction: Why Pictures Beat Tables
Imagine looking at 1,000 numbers in a column.

Now imagine seeing a graph that shows:
- A rising trend  
- A sudden drop  
- A clear comparison  

Which one is easier to understand?

Humans spot patterns visually much faster than through raw data.  
That’s why visualization matters.

---

## Why Plotting Is Important
Data visualization helps you:

1. **Identify trends** quickly  
2. **Compare categories** clearly  
3. **Communicate insights** effectively  

Before building complex models, you often visualize data first.

---

## From Known to New: Tables → Visual Insights
### What You Already Know
You can:
- Load data  
- Inspect rows  
- Calculate averages  

But numbers alone don’t show trends easily.

### The Visualization Step
Plots convert data into:
- Shapes  
- Lines  
- Points  
- Bars  

These make patterns obvious.

---

## Core Plot Types
### 1. Line Plots
Used to show trends over time or continuous data.

- Great for time series  
- Shows upward or downward movement  
- Connects data points  

*Think: “How does something change?”*

---

### 2. Scatter Plots
Used to show relationships between two variables.

- Each point represents one observation  
- Helps detect correlation  
- Reveals clusters or outliers  

*Think: “Do these two variables relate?”*

---

### 3. Bar Charts
Used to compare categories.

- Each bar represents a group  
- Height shows value  
- Easy for comparisons  

*Think: “Which category is bigger?”*

---

## Labeling and Titles
A good plot always includes:

- **Title** → what the graph shows  
- **X-axis label** → what horizontal values represent  
- **Y-axis label** → what vertical values represent  

Without labels, plots become confusing.

---

## Basic Aesthetics
Small design choices improve clarity:

- Clear axis spacing  
- Proper scaling  
- Clean formatting  
- Avoiding clutter  

The goal is clarity—not decoration.

---

## Putting It All Together
A typical workflow:
1. Load data  
2. Choose the appropriate plot type  
3. Add labels and title  
4. Adjust simple aesthetics  
5. Interpret the pattern  

Visualization turns analysis into insight.
