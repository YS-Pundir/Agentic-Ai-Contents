## Introduction: What Is Artificial Intelligence?
Think about a food delivery app.  
A normal app follows fixed rules: *if you click X, show Y*.

Now imagine an app that **learns** what you like, predicts when you’ll order, and suggests meals without being told exactly how.

That learning and adapting ability is **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**.

**AI** is software designed to perform tasks that usually need human intelligence, like learning, reasoning, or decision-making.

---

## Why Does AI Matter?
AI matters because it changes *how* software behaves.

1. **Less manual rules**  
   You don’t need to hard-code every decision. The system learns patterns on its own.

2. **Better decisions at scale**  
   AI can analyze huge amounts of data faster than humans.

3. **Smarter user experiences**  
   Apps feel personalized instead of generic.

---

## From Known to New: Software → AI
### The Traditional Way (Painful)
Imagine writing rules like:
- If marks > 90 → Grade A  
- If marks > 80 → Grade B  
- If marks > 70 → Grade C  

You must predict *every* condition in advance.

### The AI Way (Smarter)
Instead, you show the system **past data** (marks + grades).  
The system learns the pattern and predicts grades for new students.

This shift, from fixed rules to learning from data, is the core difference.

---

## Core Components of the AI Landscape
### 1. Artificial Intelligence (AI)
The **umbrella term**.  
Anything that makes machines act “intelligent” falls here.

*Example:* Chatbots, recommendation systems, fraud detection.

---

### 2. Machine Learning (ML)
A **subset of AI** where systems learn from data instead of rules.

*Example:*  
- Email spam detection  
- Netflix recommendations  

---

### 3. Generative AI (GenAI)
A **subset of ML** that creates **new content**.

*Example:*  
- Writing text  
- Generating images  
- Creating code  

Instead of just predicting, it **produces**.

---

## AI in Everyday Life (You’re Already Using It)
- **Google Maps** → predicts traffic  
- **Instagram** → curates your feed  
- **Amazon** → recommends products  
- **Voice assistants** → understand speech  

You don’t “turn on” AI, it quietly works in the background.

---

## The Road Ahead: Where AI Is Going
- More **automation** in routine tasks  
- AI as a **co-pilot**, not a replacement  
- Increased focus on **ethics and safety**  
- Wider use beyond tech: healthcare, education, finance  

AI is moving from “cool feature” to **core infrastructure**.
