# Pre-read: Prompt Engineering, Business Use Cases & AI Frameworks

## The "Why" — A Story You Already Know

Imagine you just hired a brand-new assistant at your office. Super smart, incredibly fast, can read thousands of pages in seconds. But on their very first day, you walk up and say *"Handle it."* — and walk away.

What happens? Chaos. The assistant has no idea what *"it"* means, who to call, what format to use, or what *not* to do.

This is exactly what happens every time someone types a vague message into ChatGPT and gets a useless answer back. The AI is not bad — the **instruction was bad**.

---

## The Problem — What If You Were the Manager of an AI?

Think about this: companies like Swiggy, Flipkart, and HDFC Bank are now running entire **customer support teams powered by AI**. These bots answer lakhs of customer queries every single day — refunds, order tracking, complaints, everything.

Now here is the real question: **Who writes the instructions that make these bots behave correctly?**

- How does Swiggy's bot know to *always apologise first* before giving a refund answer?
- How does HDFC's bot know it should *never share account details*, even if the customer insists?
- How does Flipkart's product listing tool know to write in a *fun, casual tone* for Gen Z shoppers?

Someone had to **craft those instructions very carefully**. And that skill — that exact skill — is what today's session is about.

---

## The Solution — Meet "Prompt Engineering"

**Prompt Engineering** (in simple words: *the art of telling AI exactly what you want, how you want it*) is the most in-demand skill in the AI industry right now.

It is not coding. It is not mathematics. It is **communication** — but with a machine that listens perfectly if you speak its language.

Today's session will teach you:
- How to write instructions that get the AI to behave like a pro every single time.
- How real companies map these instructions to actual business problems.
- And how tools like **LangChain, CrewAI, and AutoGen** act as the "team managers" that put multiple AI agents to work together — like a real office.

---

## The Simple Analogy — Think of an Auto-Rickshaw Driver

You want to go somewhere. You have two choices for what you say:

- **Option A:** *"Bhaiya, chalo."*
- **Option B:** *"Bhaiya, HSR Sector 4, 5th Main, near the bakery, shortest route please — I have a meeting in 10 minutes."*

Option A gets you a confused driver who might take the longest route possible. Option B gets you exactly where you need to go, on time.

**AI works the same way.** A vague prompt = a generic, unhelpful answer. A sharp, structured prompt = an answer you can actually use in real work. This session will show you the exact formula — called **C-I-F-C** — that separates a weak prompt from a powerful one.

## What's Next — After This Session, You Will Be Able To

- Write a **System Prompt** that gives any AI a specific personality, tone, and set of rules — just like training a new employee.
- Use **Role Prompting** to instantly make an AI answer like a CA, a doctor, a career counsellor, or a tech CEO.
- Apply the **C-I-F-C formula** (Context, Instruction, Format, Constraints) to write prompts that get *ready-to-use* outputs every time.
- Ask AI for answers in **tables, JSON, bullet points, or step-by-step guides** — whichever format your actual work needs.
- Identify **real business use cases** where AI agents are already saving companies crores of rupees.
